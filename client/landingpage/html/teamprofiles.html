With 83 teams from 34 countries all competing in the Aramco STEM Racing World Finals 2025, there's some fantastic stories of innovation, dedication and talent to celebrate

TO1:EXCELERATE RACING COUNTRY: SINGAPORE SCHOOL: XCL WORLD ACADEMY

XCLerate Racing, from Singapore, is a six-member team aged 17. Formed to represent Singapore at the 2023 World Finals, they became National Champions in 2024. Their name combines “accelerate” and “XCL,” their school sponsor. Their car, XCL-Lithium, is named for reliability and solid performance, inspired by the Greek word “lithos.”

Development began after the 2024 Nationals, with six months of simulation and testing. Challenges included balancing IB studies with competition, solved through shared schedules and communication tools. Innovations include airflow redirection using wheel inertia and Assetto Corsa mods for sim racing. XCLerate Racing is unique for its strong school identity and national representation. Their proudest moment was winning Nationals with only two members present. Dream careers include engineering, fintech, and sustainable technologies. Their favourite moment was being interviewed on national radio, sharing their journey and inspiring others.

@xclerate_racing​

T02: CHANGE HOLDINGS Hayabusa Racing Country: JAPAN School: Independent Team

Based in Minato-ku, Japan, Hayabusa Racing is an independent team formed in January 2024. Led by Rona, a returning World Finals competitor, the team comprises six male students aged 16–17. Without school backing, they became Japan’s first fully sponsor-funded team. The name “Hayabusa” reflects speed and precision, inspired by the peregrine falcon and Japan’s bullet train. Their car, HB03, evolved from prototypes “Thunderbolt” and “Beyonder.”

Development began earlier this year, with major obstacles including low sponsorship response and lack of workspace. They overcame these through A/B testing, networking, and professional guidance. Hayabusa Racing stands out for its unique marketing, project management, and engineering approach, including genetic algorithms and race-start devices. Their mission is to promote project-based learning in Japan. Highlights include visiting Honda’s Sakura Factory and meeting F1 drivers. Their dream careers span engineering, aerospace, and entrepreneurship.

@_hayabusaracing​

T03: Ascent Country: Germany School: Gymnasium Grootmoor​

Ascent, from Gymnasium Grootmoor in Hamburg, Germany, is a six-member team aged 15–17. Formed in August 2024, they quickly rose through the ranks, winning regional and national awards including fastest car and best CAD. Their name, “Ascent,” reflects their goal to climb through competition levels and improve daily. Their car, Arrow3, continues a legacy of speed and precision. Though they don’t have a mascot, their logo features an upward-pointing arrow. Development began after the German National Finals in May. Funding was a challenge, but they secured sponsors in time. Innovations include an air-powered ball bearing testing system and a pit display racing game inspired by the Singapore Grand Prix. Ascent is unique for their clear organisation and optimistic approach, despite being in their first STEM Racing season.

@ascent.racing​

T04: SBA Unity RacING Country: UNITED KINGDOM School: Scarborough University Technical College

SBA Unity Racing, from Scarborough, UK, is a dedicated team representing Scarborough Business Ambassadors. Comprising students with a passion for STEM and motorsport, they have steadily progressed through regional and national competitions. Their car, developed through months of teamwork and innovation, reflects their commitment to precision and performance. The team overcame challenges such as funding constraints and technical setbacks by fostering resilience and collaboration.

Notable innovations include advanced engineering solutions and a focus on sustainability. What makes SBA Unity Racing unique is their strong community spirit and drive to inspire future generations in Scarborough. Their proudest moment was qualifying for the World Finals, a testament to their hard work and determination. The team aspires to careers in engineering, business, and technology, and their favourite STEM Racing moment was celebrating their achievements together, united by a shared vision for success.

@sba.unity.racing

T05: Absolute Racing Country: China

Absolute Racing, from Beijing, China, is a six-member all-male team aged 16–17. Formed as a successor to Wings Racing, they carry a decade-long legacy in STEM Racing. Their name, “Absolute,” translates from the Chinese word “jue dui,” symbolising certainty and timeless excellence. Their car, Red Swallow, is inspired by the swallow’s speed and cultural significance, with its tail design resembling the bird’s silhouette. Their mascot, the Beijing Swallow, appears throughout their branding.

​Development spanned 93 days, with obstacles including schoolwork and TOEFL exams. They adapted by prioritising tasks and supporting each other. Innovations include integrating modern project management with traditional Chinese strategy, wind tunnel testing, and mathematical modelling. Absolute Racing is unique for its mission to define car culture in China. Their proudest moment was connecting with Jim Glickenhaus during sponsorship outreach. Dream roles include dual careers in car design and engineering. Their favourite moment was winning at Nationals.

@absoluteracing_sdsz

T06: Ad Astra Country: Australia SCHOOL: Brighton Grammar School

Ad Astra, from Brighton Grammar School in Australia, is a six-member team aged 14–16. Their name, Latin for “to the stars,” reflects ambition and resilience. Formed in late 2023, they’ve rapidly progressed from State Champions to World Finalists. Their car, Stella, symbolises guidance and excellence. Though they lack a mascot, their innovations shine - most notably, a Rear Wing Design Competition involving primary schools, now featured in their pit display.

​Funding was a major hurdle, overcome through teamwork and outreach. Ad Astra’s strength lies in their unity and rapid ascent through competition levels. Their favourite experience has been meeting diverse teams and mentors across Australia. Dream careers include F1 engineering, strategy, and mechanics. Their journey from States to Nationals to Worlds in one campaign showcases their determination and collaborative spirit.

@adastraracing

T07: ALPHA SCUDERIA SESI SENAI SC Country: Brazil SCHOOL: Escola SESI Criciúma​​

ALPHA SCUDERIA, from Criciúma, Brazil, is a veteran team with a rich history in STEM Racing. Competing since 2020, they’ve earned awards for engineering, sustainability, and collaboration. Their name redefines “Alpha” as empathetic leadership, inspired by modern wolf pack research. Their car, W25-S, continues a naming tradition tied to year and location. Their mascot, Alphie - a pink and purple wolf - adds personality to their branding. Over six months, they developed their car while navigating stakeholder engagement, storytelling challenges, and supplier coordination. Innovations span software, engineering prototypes, and sustainability projects.

ALPHA SCUDERIA is unique for its legacy-driven approach and social impact. Their proudest moment was winning the National Finals 2025 after years of striving. Team members aspire to careers in engineering, marketing, and communication. They’re driven by a desire to leave a lasting legacy in STEM Racing.

@​alpha.scuderia

T08: Daedalus Racing Country: Greece SCHOOL: Mandoulides Schools, Erasmeios Greek and German School, Ekpedeftiki Anagennisi

Daedalus Racing, based in Attica, Greece, is a collaborative team formed from three schools separated by over 500 kilometres. Despite the distance, they’ve built strong unity and a shared passion for motorsport. Their name, inspired by the myth of Daedalus, reflects creativity and ambition. Their car, also named Daedalus, symbolises speed and invention. Development began in April, with obstacles including limited access to race tracks. They overcame this by creating a Python programme to predict race times.

​Innovations include a regulation-checking software, performance predictor, and smart testing tools. Daedalus Racing is unique for its outreach work, inspiring hundreds of students through STEM presentations and events. Their proudest achievement is hosting ten educational sessions and partnering with Circuit 27, Greece’s largest racetrack. Dream careers range from engineering to sports medicine and design. Their favourite moment was connecting with karting experts and building friendships through STEM Racing.

@daedalus_.racing

Their name honours the Greek goddess of speed, reflecting resilience and cultural pride. Their car, Goddess Atalanta, was developed over three months. Challenges included limited funding and suppliers, overcome through strategic resource management and international sourcing.

​Innovations include forward-lofted front wing endplates to optimise finish-line detection and airflow. Atalanta Racing is unique for its identity-driven approach and commitment to excellence. Their proudest moment was qualifying for the World Finals. Dream careers include project management and engineering. Their favourite moment was submitting their final deliverables, marking the culmination of their efforts.

@atalanta.racing

Challenges included competition delays, technical issues, and personal emergencies, overcome through coordination and extra effort. Innovations include a new wing design, recycled uniforms, and heritage-inspired branding. NAGA Racing is unique for its cultural integration and sustainability focus. T11: APEX Elegance Country: Macau SCHOOL: Macao Science Center​

Apex Elegance, the first international STEM Racing team from Macau, was founded in August 2024. Their name blends “Apex” for precision and “Elegance” for refined execution, reflecting their design philosophy: “Simple. Complex. Elegant.” Their car, Zenith, symbolises peak performance. Their mascot, a royal purple cat, adds charm and agility to their branding. Over eight months, they developed a modular pit display and sustainable materials, solving transport challenges with clever design.

​Their innovations include recycled tea leaf boards, parameterised CAD, and analytics-driven project management. Apex Elegance stands out for merging Macau’s racing heritage with modern sustainability. Their proudest moment was outperforming top teams with an unfinished car that ran the fastest time of the day. Team members aim for careers in engineering and design. Their journey proves that resilience and preparation can turn setbacks into triumphs.

T12: Blitz Racing Country: Ireland SCHOOL: Marist College Athlone​

Blitz Racing, from Athlone, Ireland, is a three-member all-male team aged 17–18. Formed in late 2024, they won the Irish National Finals and Best Pit Display. Their name, “Blitz,” means lightning in German, symbolising speed and power. Their car, Sionna MK.2, is named after the Irish goddess of the River Shannon, connecting their design to local heritage.

​Development involved over 130 hours of CAD modelling and extensive testing. Innovations include a unique three-ramp front wing and strategic virtual cargo placement. Blitz Racing is unique for its bold branding and cultural integration. Their proudest moment was winning Nationals. Dream careers include aerospace engineering, medicine, and design. Their favourite moment was hearing their name announced as champions, validating their hard work.

@blitzracing2024

T13: SVT Racing Country: Thailand SCHOOL: King's College International School Bangkok

SVT Racing, from Bangkok, Thailand, is a five-member team aged 16–17. Formed through the FOBISIA STEM competition, they placed in the top three at Nationals. Their name comes from the physics equation s = vt, symbolising speed and efficiency. Their car, Black Necromancer, is inspired by Star Wars and reflects their ambition to create an impossibly fast design.

​Development spanned over seven months, with challenges in engineering skill gaps, resolved through training. Innovations include underbody fins applying Bernoulli’s principle for improved traction. SVT Racing is unique for its close-knit team dynamic and shared classroom history. Their proudest moment was producing their first World Finals car. Dream careers include theoretical physics and engineering. Their favourite moment was drawing inspiration from Team Infinitude’s world record car, motivating them to push boundaries.

@svtracingf1

TONALTECH, from Tonalá, Mexico, is a five-member team aged 17–19. Competing since 2016, they’ve won six national championships and two international awards. Their name combines “Tonalá” and “Tech,” reflecting their mission to inspire youth through technology. Their car, Maxa, is named after the Wixárika word for deer, symbolising speed and cultural heritage.

​Development began in April, with challenges in weight reduction, solved through extensive testing. Innovations include rear chevrons to reduce turbulence and improve airflow. TONALTECH is unique for its community commitment, aiming to leave a legacy for future teams. Their proudest moment was receiving the FIA Women in Motorsport Award and seeing their community celebrate their success. Dream careers include automotive engineering and racing. Their favourite moment was racing together and feeling the pride of representing their region.

@tonaltech
